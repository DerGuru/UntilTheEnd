# Royal Road — Genres & Tags (Stand April 2026)

## Genres (max. 4)

| Genre | Beschreibung |
|-------|-------------|
| Action | Action stories involve exciting and dramatic scenes. Character movements are described in detail, such as in combat scenes. Martial Arts is a subgenre of action. |
| Adventure | Adventure stories involve the main character facing a challenge or series of challenges outside of their ordinary life. Plot events are central to the story. Adventure stories often involve danger and action scenes. |
| Comedy | Comedy stories are intended to amuse the reader through humour. Subgenres include satire and parody. |
| Contemporary | Contemporary stories take place in the modern era, with setting, plot, and characters that seem believable as a true story. |
| Drama | Dramatic stories focus on emotional themes and interpersonal relationships. These stories feature realistic characters and their emotional development. |
| Fantasy | Fantasy stories involve supernatural elements such as magic and mythological creatures. These imaginative stories can depict the real world with fantasy elements (low fantasy), or take place in a completely reimagined fantasy world (high fantasy). |
| Historical | Historical stories take place in a setting located in the past. Stories will include accurate details about the manners and socioeconomic conditions of the era. |
| Horror | Horror stories are intended to inspire feelings of horror or terror in the reader. They often focus on elements of death, afterlife, and evil. They are often supernatural, but do not have to be. |
| Mystery | Mystery stories revolving around characters attempting to figure out a puzzling crime or perplexing situation. |
| Psychological | Psychological stories focus on the inner action of the characters, including motives, thoughts, and emotions from which plot events result. Characterization is particularly important to the story. Psychological thriller is a subgenre characterizing unstable psychological states. |
| Romance | Romance stories focus on the relationship and romantic love between people. Romance is a driving factor of the story. |
| Satire | A genre in which vices, follies, abuses, and shortcomings are held up to ridicule, ideally with the intent of shaming individuals, corporations, government, or society itself into improvement. |
| Sci-fi | Science fiction stories take place in imaginative, futuristic settings. Common elements include space travel, advanced technology, time travel, parallel universes, and extraterrestrial life. |
| Short Story | Short stories are pieces intended to be read in one sitting. Because of its condensed form, short stories often have brief or skipped introductions, as well as open-ended or abrupt conclusions. Plot is condensed, and these stories are less complex than full novels. |
| Thriller | Stories that focus on suspense and high-stakes, plot-driven narratives. |
| Tragedy | Tragedy is a form of drama that invokes human suffering. Classic tragic stories are intended to provide catharsis, or purging of negative emotion in the audience. Stories involve a noble character brought low by fate or circumstance. |

## Tags

| Tag | Beschreibung |
|-----|-------------|
| Anti-Hero Lead | Anti-Hero Lead stories depict leading characters that lack the usual heroic qualities such as idealism, courage, or morality. Usually, anti-heroes blur the lines between a hero and a villain, depicting the characteristics of both. |
| Anti-Villain Lead | Stories featuring characters who take on the role of the villain but retain sympathetic, moral, or understandable motives. |
| Apocalypse | The main character's home world, universe, or equivalent, faces the threat of imminent destruction, and the main character is working to stop it or survive through it. |
| Artificial Intelligence | A subgenre focusing on characters or major actors that are advanced AI. |
| Attractive Lead | The main character of this novel is exceptionally attractive — to the point where it deserves an extra tag on the story due to the sheer significance. |
| Chivalry | Stories that focus on themes of family loyalty, honor, upright honesty, integrity, and chivalry. |
| Competing Love Interest | Multiple characters are vying for the MC's attention, but the MC will only choose one. |
| Cozy | Stories that give the reader a feeling of warmth, comfort, and relaxation, with minimal stress or conflict. |
| Crafting | Stories that center around the creation, refinement, and mastery of items, potions, artifacts, or other crafted works. |
| Cultivation | A type of Chinese martial arts novel that typically focuses on cultivation and a quest for immortality. |
| Cyberpunk | Cyberpunk is a subgenre of science fiction that focuses on technological advancements themselves. They usually feature a change of social order because of said advancements. |
| Deck Building | Stories set in worlds where power systems revolve around cards, where characters gain abilities or strength based on the cards they possess. |
| Dungeon Core | Stories focus on a sentient dungeon or an entity that controls one. |
| Dungeon Crawler | A type of fiction that takes place or focuses on loot-bearing Dungeons similar to the eponymous RPG. |
| Dystopia | A society where the common person's living conditions are undesirable or frightening, often with a totalitarian government/authority. |
| Female Lead | Female Lead stories have, unsurprisingly, a leading female human character, or of any particular sapient humanoid race. |
| First Contact | A story focusing on the first meeting between two or more species or societies and the events that occur because of it. |
| GameLit | Novels set in game-like worlds of any game genre. Does not need to heavily focus on visual statistics. |
| Gender Bender | Gender Bender fictions utilise the theme of gender swapping. The gender swapping can be temporary or permanent, full or partial, and may or may not be the theme based on which the fiction revolves. |
| Genetically Engineered | Characters are genetically modified to be superior to their original species. |
| Grimdark | Grimdark is a subgenre or a way to describe the tone, style or setting of speculative fiction (especially fantasy) that is, depending on the definition used, markedly dystopian or amoral, or particularly violent or realistic. |
| Hard Sci-fi | Uses theoretically possible science and attempts to explain how the science works; does not include fantasy elements. |
| High Fantasy | Defined by its epic nature, High Fantasy takes place in an alternate world with its own rules and laws. |
| Kingdom Building | Stories where the main character is focused on building a long-standing base, usually a kingdom or an equivalent. |
| Lesbian Romance | Stories that include characters who are interested in or pursuing lesbian relationships. |
| LitRPG | Novels where linear progression, such as levels, are main themes of the story. Almost always shows stat boxes, EXP gain, and other notifications. |
| Local Protagonist | A character born and raised within their own world and time. They possess no external knowledge beyond their world, and that world has not undergone any drastic, unpredictable changes. The character's understanding is limited to what they learn within the confines of their own reality. |
| Low Fantasy | A type of fantasy that takes place in the real world, putting fantastical elements in an otherwise natural setting. |
| Magic | Magic fictions contain the use or means of magic (as charms or spells) that have supernatural power over natural forces. Practitioners are commonly referred to as mages (or magi), witches and warlocks, wizards, and sorcerers. Most commonly used in fantasy fictions. |
| Magical Girl | Stories where an ordinary person (usually a young girl) is granted magical powers and a mission, often involving battles against monsters unseen by the ordinary world. |
| Magitech | Stories where magic and technology are combined, where technology produces magical effects and/or is powered by magic. |
| Male Gay Romance | Stories that include characters who are interested in or pursuing gay (male) relationships. |
| Male Lead | Male Lead stories have, you guessed it, a leading male human character, or of any particular sapient humanoid race. |
| Martial Arts | Martial Arts stories commonly describe to great detail the intricacies of western or eastern combat. |
| Mecha | Stories featuring giant piloted robots, usually (but not always) humanoid in shape. |
| Modern Knowledge | Stories where the main character uses knowledge from modern Earth, another world, or a future timeline to advance a less-developed society. |
| Monster Evolution | Stories where the protagonist begins life as a monster, beast, or non-human creature and grows stronger through evolution. |
| Multiple Lead Characters | Multiple Lead Characters fictions depict stories said from points of view of a number of characters, the actual number varying between two and too many to count. The characters can be of any gender and race. |
| Multiple Lovers | Multiple Lovers (previously Harem) stories depict multiple characters who have romantic interest toward the lead. Sometimes, it includes polyamorous relationships. |
| Mythos | A type of fiction focusing on mythology, such as deities and elements of the divine, or the occult. |
| Non-Human Lead | Non-Human Lead stories have main character(s) not be human, whether partially or completely. The term may include or exclude humanoid fantastical races. The main theme of said stories can be the loss or redefinition of the term human or humane. |
| Non-Humanoid Lead | Stories where the main character is not humanoid. They are NOT human, nor elf/dwarf, or any other near-human species. |
| Otome | Stories where a character (usually female) enters the world of a game or novel and tries to avoid a bad ending. |
| Portal Fantasy / Isekai | Features a fantastical world that is entered through a portal or some other means, and differs from the real world. In Japan, this type of work is known as isekai (different world). |
| Post Apocalyptic | The world has been ruined, and this is the story of the survivors. |
| Progression | Characters work to improve themselves over time rather than acquiring rapid growth, usually through training, grinding, studying, and/or introspection. |
| Reader Interactive | Reader Interactive uses reader inputs (usually polls) to define the progression of the novel. They can also be "choose your own adventure" stories, told in second person narrative. |
| Reincarnation | Reincarnation utilises a common theme of a person's life restarting or beginning anew after their death, not necessarily connected to the teachings of hinduism and buddhism. |
| Romance Subplot | Stories with romance as a subplot, the driving factor of the story isn't romance. |
| Ruling Class | Royalty, nobles, or characters in a body of governance, playing a major part in the story. Typically involving a power struggle. |
| School Life | School Life stories usually place their characters in a setting regarding education and schooling. The main character(s) are usually students in the institutions. School Life may be a primary theme, or can just span a portion of the story. |
| Secret Identity | Secret Identity fictions use the theme of subterfuge and secrecy employed by one or more characters (main or otherwise) to pose as different personae. The secret identity is usually vital to the story or setting. |
| Slice of Life | Slice of Life stories usually depict snippets or parts of one or more characters' life, which may or may not be connected to the main plot themselves. There doesn't even have to be a main plot in a traditional sense. |
| Soft Sci-fi | Uses futuristic-type science, but doesn't explain how the technology works and the science isn't necessarily realistic or feasible. |
| Space Opera | A subgenre of science fiction that focuses on space warfare, interplanetary battles, futuristic weapons, and is usually set in outer space. May include subthemes of chivalry and romance. |
| Sports | Sports fictions focus on competitive displays of physical or mental prowess. The sports can be real or imaginary, but usually have to contain a self-explanatory set of rules. |
| Steampunk | A subgenre of science fiction inspired by and incorporating 19th-century industrial steam-powered machinery and technology, usually set in an alternate Victorian Era or the Wild West. |
| Strategy | Strategy stories depict characters or setting that do not rely on pure physical prowess, combat, or clashing, instead depicting tactical and critical thinking. |
| Strong Lead | Strong Lead stories depict characters which are unnaturally or unusually potent compared to the norm of the setting they are in. They can be either physically and/or mentally superior, or have a different advantage altogether. |
| Super Heroes | A subgenre focusing on people with supernatural abilities, or ones that focus on fighting the evil of the universe, usually in costumes. |
| Supernatural | Supernatural stories contain an unnatural element which cannot be found in reality. Common themes include magic, zombies, vampires, werewolves, dragons, and other fae. Supernatural fictions are usually alternate world or low fiction stories, where the main setting is in the actual contemporary Earth. |
| Survival | Stories that focus on the character's struggle to survive. |
| System Invasion | Stories where a game-like System appears in the main character's world, often accompanied by the arrival of magic, monsters, and the collapse of modern technology. |
| Technologically Engineered | Characters are enhanced or created through technology, such as cyborgs or robots. |
| Time Loop | Characters relive the same time period through a loop; may or may not involve alternate timelines. |
| Time Travel | Characters directly travel through time, possibly within the same timeline, alternate timelines, or even creating new timelines. |
| Tower | Stories centered around towers that must be climbed to survive. These towers are often filled with challenges, monsters, and rewards. |
| Urban Fantasy | Works that feature fantastical or supernatural elements in an urban setting, which may or may not be contemporary. |
| Villainous Lead | As opposed to Anti-Hero, villain leads are not benevolent. Usually features protagonists with immoral or evil goals. |
| Virtual Reality | Virtual Reality stories often incorporate headsets or full-body machines that simulate a different reality or depict moving a human consciousness entirely into a virtual or digital environment. The story can span both the actual and the virtual reality. |
| War and Military | Fictions that focus on war and the military life of soldiers. |
| Wuxia | Based on Chinese literature wuxia (Martial Heroes) features itinerant warriors of ancient China, often depicted as capable of superhuman feats of martial arts. |

## Content Warnings

| Warning | Beschreibung |
|---------|-------------|
| AI-Assisted Content | The author has used an AI tool for editing or proofreading. The story thus reflects the author's creativity and structure, but it may use the AI's voice and tone. There may be some negligible amount of snippets generated by AI. |
| AI-Generated Content | The story was generated using an AI tool; the author prompted and directed the process, and edited the result. |
| Graphic Violence | Detailed descriptions of violent acts, including but not limited to physical harm, bloodshed, mutilation, or any other explicit depictions of violence that may be considered disturbing or excessively graphic. |
| Profanity | Excessive or obscene swearing and cursing. |
| Sensitive Content | Descriptions of torture, slavery, substance abuse, mental illness, addiction, mentions of rape, self-harm or any other content that may be considered traumatizing or mature that does not fall under the warning tags. This content is not glorified. |
| Sexual Content | Explicit sexual content (text only), descriptive sex scenes. |
