You said it again. Slower. Shorter. Each sentence weighted with the patience of a man who is trying very hard not to be angry at someone who doesn't deserve anger.

The anomalies were caused by those cultivators and me. The qi signature was always mine. Reality adjusts to their and my presence. The researchers who discovered this were erased. Something in the cultivators and me doesn't want anyone to know.

I listened. I understood. Again. The logic was flawless. The evidence was arranged in the sand, redrawn, simpler this time, your lines heavier, as if pressing harder might make the knowledge stick.

I nodded.

"Okay," I said.

"Okay?"

"Okay. I hear you. I understand."

Your breath released. Only halfway. You were watching me now the way you watch a candle in a draft, waiting for the flicker.

Two minutes.

That's how long it lasted. Two minutes of understanding, two minutes of clarity, two minutes during which I held the shape of the truth in my mind like water held in cupped hands. Present, visible, undeniable and slowly, inevitably, seeping through the cracks between my fingers.

The first thing to go was the connection between the anomalies and me. One moment it was clear and the next it was a proposition, a maybe, a thing someone had suggested that sounded plausible but wasn't quite.

Then the researchers. Six of them. Or had it been five? The scrolls said... what did the scrolls say? I could see them, there, in the firelight. They said something about qi obeying will. A phrase. Someone else's phrase. Important, but —

Then the map. The tightening path. You had shown me and I'd seen the pattern, but now it was — was it a pattern? Or was it coincidence? We'd walked south because south was where the ruins were and ruins cluster in certain geological formations and —

Gone.

Like a dream four minutes after waking. The contours remained. Something important, something large, something that had filled the space behind my eyes with a pressure that meant truth. But the content had dissolved. Not all at once, this time. Piece by piece. A dismantling. Methodical.

I looked at you.

Your eyes were different. Angry. Deeper than irritation, something harder, held tight.

"How long?" I asked.

"Two minutes."

"Was it the same? What you said?"

"Word for word."

"And I —"

"Understood. Perfectly. Said 'okay.' Looked like you meant it." Your voice was flat. Forcibly flat, the kind of control that costs something. "And then your eyes changed."

"Changed how?"

"Like someone lowering a wick. From behind your eyes. I watched the understanding leave your face, Yun. I watched it get taken."

Silence.

The fire had burned lower. The scrolls still lay in their rows. The sand drawings, your third set now, redrawn with less precision and more force, visible in the dying light like a language being forgotten in real time.

"One more time," you said.

"Why?"

"Because." You stood. Found a new stick, thinner, sharper. Your movements had a quality of controlled urgency, the body language of someone who knows they're fighting something and has decided to fight harder rather than surrender. "Because this time I'm going to watch for it. I'm going to see the exact moment it happens."

You repeated it a third time.

Each word a nail driven with precision into the same spot, the same board, the same argument. You didn't draw in the sand now. You spoke directly, facing me, your eyes locked on mine, watching — I could feel it — for the exact expression, the precise moment, the flicker that would tell you when the erasure began.

I change reality. My qi is autonomous. Something in me erases understanding. Others had this. All were stopped.

I listened.

I understood.

I watched you watching me.

And then —

It was like a hand closing over a flame. Gentle, even. The way you'd close a book, the way you'd draw a curtain, the way you'd fold a page. A shutting. A covering. A shadow that fell across the interior of my mind and extinguished the thing that had been burning there, the comprehension, the pattern, the truth.

For a fraction of a second, less than a heartbeat, both states existed simultaneously. Knowing and not-knowing, layered on top of each other like layers of ice over deep water and I could see through both and one of them was clear, lit and true. The other was dark, empty and safe. And the dark one was winning, expanding, covering the light with the gentle, irresistible certainty of an ocean tide covering a beach.

Then it was gone.

I sat by the fire. The copied scrolls were spread out before us. You had drawn something in the earth.

"What?" I said.

Your face.

I will remember your face. Even through whatever was being done to my mind, even through the erasure and the forgetting and the gentle, persistent theft of understanding, I will remember your face in that moment. 

It was the face of a man who had just watched something terrible happen to someone and could do nothing to stop it.

Neither angry nor frustrated. Horrified.

"She can't hear herself," you said to yourself, picking up stones and throwing them into the darkness. You talked to the fire and to the dark that surrounded our small circle of light like a patient audience. "She says it. She means it. And then it's taken. She can't hear her own truth."

"Xu Ran." My voice. Small. Wrong. I didn't know why. "What's happening?"

You didn't answer. You sat down. Picked up your notebook. Your hands, your steady archivist's hands, were trembling. You opened to a blank page. Wrote.

Date. Time.

*Episode 1: explained pattern. She understood. 10 seconds later, forgotten. She asked "what were you saying."*

*Episode 2: repeated explanation. She understood. 2 minutes later, forgotten. Piece by piece. Systematic. Like someone disassembling a structure from inside.*

*Episode 3: repeated explanation. She understood. During erasure: her eyes dimmed. Active process. Like something reaching in. DELETION.*

Your pen stopped. You set it down. You looked at me.

The leaves above us moved. No wind. The stones around the fire hummed, a low vibration I felt in my teeth rather than heard.

Memory fragments surfaced. Just debris. An image. A tree that had been on the left and was now on the right. A face. Someone, somewhere, mouth open, mid-word. A feeling. Warmth in stone. A sound. Silence where there should have been an echo.

Shards. Splinters. Pieces of a mirror that had been shattered and scattered. Each piece reflected something different and none of them fit together. Trying to fit them together produced a headache so sharp that it crowded out thought.

I pressed my hands against my temples and squeezed.

"Something is wrong," I said.

"Yes."

"I don't know what."

"I do."

"Something is — there's a — I can feel..." The words dissolved. Language failing, the way language fails when the thing you're trying to describe is actively preventing its own description. "There's a gap. In me. Something that should be there and isn't."

"I know what it is," you said. Quiet. Steady. The steadiness of someone standing on a ledge and refusing to look down. "But you won't remember me telling you."

"Try."

"I've tried. Three times."

"Try again."

"No." You closed the notebook. Held it against your chest. "Not tonight. Not like this. Whatever is doing this, your own protection, the thing that keeps you alive, whatever it is, it's stronger when you push. It fights harder. And I can't fight it for you."

The leaves settled. The stones stopped humming. The clearing returned to normal, or what passed for normal.

I sat in the quiet. The gap pressed against my sternum. The empty space where something had been. Three times and three times removed. I couldn't see into it. I couldn't name it. But I could feel its edges. Sharp, clean, precise. Whatever had made this cut knew exactly what it was doing.

"Something in me," I said. Half question, half statement. The verbal equivalent of reaching for a door handle in the dark.

"Yes," you said. "Something in you."

I looked at my hands. They were clean. The fire reflected off my palms in warm light and there was nothing wrong with my hands, nothing visible, nothing that would tell a stranger what lived behind my eyes and what it was willing to do to keep me safe.

To keep me ignorant.

Same thing, I suspected.

Same thing.
