You picked up your bowl. Washed it and set it by the pack.

You meditated. Louder than usual — the volume increase that indicated the noise-need had intensified. The mantras were faster. The humming more insistent. The breathing was theatrical in the way that desperation made things theatrical.

I sat. The fire between us. Your meditation filling the space with sound that was now —

I knew the rhythm.

The rise and fall. The contour that was not melody but resembled it. The sequence that repeated every forty seconds. I knew it. Not from study. From exposure. I knew when your breathing would deepen. I knew when the mantra would conclude and the humming would bridge. I knew when the humming would accelerate and the next mantra would begin.

The immortal who didn't care had accidentally memorized a stranger's meditation schedule. This was either the most pathetic thing that had happened in my existence or the second most pathetic, the first being that I noticed I had done it.

I noticed that I knew.

Something in me still learned. Still absorbed. Still converted proximity into knowledge and knowledge into —

Familiarity. 

The future contained a predictable element — your meditation rhythm. The future contained something I knew about before it happened and the knowing-before was —

The first stirring of a mind that had decided the future was a space that contained things rather than a space that was empty.

The rhythm continued. The fire burned. The night was cold. But that cold was external, because I was one degree warmer than before. The difference between zero and one.

You slept, eventually. The silence returned. The you-shaped hole in the silence that I had noticed before was still there.

You never knew I'd memorized your rhythm. Or you knew and pretended not to. Either way — it was the first thing I kept.

Keeping it felt like both progress and risk. Proximity became familiarity without asking permission. Familiarity became a thread.

I did not pull on it.

I sat in the silence. In the hole. In the one-degree-warmer-than-before.

The humming beneath my ribs continued. The substrate breathing. The echo.

Morning. The cliff behind us. Eight hundred meters of vertical stone receding as we moved — or as I moved and you followed, the dynamic changed, no longer ten paces behind, the gradient from brown to yellow to green gone.

But the movement was different.

I had a direction.

At a fork in the path — a natural fork, a real fork, not the product of frustration or impulse or the reality-editing the substrate performed — I chose left. Left because left led downhill and downhill led to the lowlands and the lowlands led to the regions where the investigation would begin.

I chose. The weighing of options, the assessment of outcomes, the selection of one over the other. Will. The old path to will — not repaired. Rewired, roughly. Something that was not quite will but close enough to serve.

Before, I had walked through forks the way water moves through rock — whichever channel was wider, whichever slope was steeper. No pause. No assessment. The feet decided, not the mind. This time was different.

You noticed the choice. The noticing was visible in your notebook — you wrote as you moved, the pen keeping rhythm that said: *fork. She stopped. Looked both directions. Chose left.* 

You did not comment. You followed left.

The path descended. Through terrain that was trees, slope, the lowland transition that mountains produced when they stopped being mountains. A real path. Worn by other feet. Not mine. The world's.

Dust was on my robes. The fine grey particles from the cliff's base, the landing zone, the place where eight hundred meters of falling had concluded in floating and settling and standing. The dust was evidence. I had not cleaned it. Deliberately. The dust was the only physical evidence that the fall had happened and that had changed everything, which needed evidence.

I would investigate.

The thought was formed — An actual thought. I would investigate what I was. I would find the places where I had survived things before — old sites, old falls, old wounds, old deaths-that-weren't-deaths. I would look for patterns. 

The humming accompanied us low beneath my ribs. The second breathing. Present the way my own breathing was present — constant, unremarkable, the background condition of being alive.

The question accompanied us as well. *What am I?* A compass. In the chest where the hand had pressed, in the space where the substrate had said *no*.

You kept behind me. Your notebook in your hand, the pen moving. Your breathing accompanied us with the slight elevation of rate that moving at elevation produced in a cultivator with a broken core.

We were two people moving away from a cliff. We looked like pilgrims. We were not pilgrims.

The path descended. We descended with it. The distance from the cliff increased. Not away-from the cliff but towards something. Towards the next thing, the first step of our investigation.

The shift was complete.

I moved. You followed. The humming continued. Two figures descending through mountain terrain toward the lowlands below.

Moving. Toward a question. And the possibility of an answer.

The dust on my robes. The humming in my chest.
