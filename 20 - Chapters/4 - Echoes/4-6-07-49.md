The clarity lasted three hours.

Three hours in which the double realization — it comes from me, it protects only me — was present and sharp. Three hours in which the understanding formed and became structural. Not a feeling — an understanding of how the thing-below-qi operated.

Then the edges softened.

Gradual. The understanding dimming like a light on a slow mechanism. I still knew I had realized something, I knew it was important — but the details blurred. I had understood exactly how the protection worked. Now I understood it approximately. An hour later, I had understood that the protection worked. The how was gone. Another hour and I had understood something about protection. The what was uncertain.

I was losing something and I was not sure what, which was probably the point.

The erasure was selective and progressive and it operated on the part of the understanding that whatever lived inside identified as threatening. The conclusion, that it came from me and protects only me, was being degraded from precision to blur. Not destroyed. Degraded. The difference was important. Previous erasures had been complete but this was only partial. As if the substrate was working against something that resisted. As if the erasure was meeting opposition.

The voice. The *not yet* voice. The other tenant.

I did not hear it again — not as words. But the resistance was its signature. It, the something inside me, erased and that voice resisted. It degraded, while the voice maintained. The tug-of-war conducted inside me between the thing that hid and the thing that kept.

By the late morning, all that remained was a direction. Inward. The compass bearing that the cliff had set and the poison experiment had confirmed. The erasure could not delete that completely because the direction existed at the level where the voice operated and it seemed stronger or at least equally strong. The contest was ongoing.

The sun came close to the zenith and there was nothing left to stop it. The original tree line was gone, reduced to splinters and debris. Beyond that, the felled trunks lay radially outward for another forty paces. Beyond those, the trees that still stood were stripped bare — trunks intact, canopies torn away, naked wood offering no shade at all. The entire basin was open sky now. The sun hit the fissured ground without filter and the temperature climbed.

You were lying in direct light. No canopy. No standing shade anywhere within seventy paces. The felled trunks cast shadows, but low and narrow. By noon, even those would shrink to nothing.

I scanned the wreckage. One of the larger trunks had fallen against another at an angle — two trees that had toppled into each other, their combined weight creating a lean-to of bark and broken wood. But the wedge of shade beneath it was large enough for a man and would hold for a few hours as the sun tracked overhead.

I moved you there. Carefully. A thin layer of qi beneath your body, shaped into a cushion between you and the ground. The qi held your weight distributed, kept the ribs from shifting, absorbed the small jolts that dragging would have produced. I guided you across the fissured earth without your body touching a single crack edge. Your head out of the sun. The shoulder elevated on a folded section of your pack. The ribs supported by the ground's angle.

You made a sound when I moved you. Not a word. A compressed exhalation — the sound of pain arriving and being refused entry into full consciousness. Your face tightened. The muscles around the eyes, the jaw. A grimace that lasted two seconds and dissolved back into the slack of sleep. It was better that you slept. Sleep was where the body did its repair work best, where the inflammation signaled without adding the complication of movement, where the ribs could begin the slow process of knitting without being asked to breathe deeply or hold weight.

I placed my hand on your chest. The midday dose. The qi thread — thin, careful, routed through the primary meridian. The tissue around the ribs received it. The inflammation had reduced measurably since the night. The swelling around the shoulder was less. Marginal improvements. But present.

I withdrew and considered.

The channels. Not the tissue, not the bones — the channels themselves. Your meridian system was wreckage, had been wreckage since I interrupted your tribulation. My fault. My intervention, my timing, my decision — and the consequence was yours to carry. A shattered core, collapsed junctions, secondary pathways scarred over in places and open in others. The qi I had been threading through them all night had followed the paths that still existed — narrow, unpredictable, but functional enough to carry the small doses I was sending.

But some of those paths were partially blocked. Cluttered. Scar tissue from the damage I had caused, like riverbeds choked with sediment. I could clear some of it. The least I could do. I had broken this system. Rebuilding it — restoring the channels to their original capacity, reconnecting junctions, reopening the core — that was beyond what I would attempt. Not here, not yet. Not on an unconscious man whose system was already stressed by broken ribs and a concussion. The risk of destabilizing what remained was too high. But clearing — removing the sediment, widening the existing paths by a fraction. 

I owed you that much.

I sent a thread along the primary meridian. Slower than the healing doses. Not depositing qi into the tissue but moving through the channel itself, feeling the walls, the constrictions, the places where the residue had narrowed the path. I found a blockage — not a full collapse but a thickening, the scar tissue reducing the channel's diameter by half. I pressed the qi against it. Gently. The way water pressed against silt. The residue shifted. A fraction. The channel widened by a margin too small to see but large enough to feel.

I moved to the next constriction. And the next. Working slowly. The way someone cleaned an old irrigation ditch — section by section. Until it was enough for now. More would stress a system already under strain. The cleared paths would make the healing doses more efficient — less resistance, better distribution, more qi reaching the tissue that needed it.

I did not touch the core. It sat at the center of the wreckage like a collapsed building — unstable, sharp-edged, the kind of structure where intervention caused secondary collapses. Whatever had broken your core had done so thoroughly. Repairing it would take time and very precise work after all your channels were repaired. Not here. Not now. Perhaps not ever. The risk was your life and the margin was very thin.

I sat back.

The sun moved. The shade from the fallen trunk shifted slowly — the geometry of shadows changing as midday approached its peak. Insects had returned in force now. Not the crickets from the night — daytime insects, the ones that operated in heat and light. Flies found the dried blood on the discarded bandage fabric. The birds had multiplied. The morning scout had been joined by others. Territorial calls. A woodpecker working one of the damaged trunks, finding the exposed wood beneath torn bark. The efficient exploitation of catastrophe. Where trees fell, insects colonized. Where insects colonized, birds followed. The destruction was already becoming a habitat.

The air smelled different now than it had at dawn. The night-cooled resin scent had given way to something warmer — heated sap from the broken trunks, the sharp sweetness of freshly exposed heartwood drying in the sun, the mineral smell of fissured earth warming. The clearing was composting in real time. The forest beginning to digest what I had done to it.

You murmured something. I turned. Your lips moved — a fragment of a word, or several, compressed by the shallow breathing that your ribs imposed. Unintelligible. The voice of someone whose mind was processing at a level that speech could not follow. Your hand twitched against your pack. The fingers curling and releasing. The involuntary movements of sleep that was not entirely peaceful.

I watched your face in the shade. The scab on the temple — darkening, drying, contracting as the tissue beneath it healed. The bruising around the left eye had developed fully now — dark purple, edged with green, the color progression that said the blood was being reabsorbed. Ugly. Functional. Normal.

Your breathing caught. A deeper intake — immediate punishment from the ribs — immediate retreat to the shallow rhythm. Your face tightened again to a grimace. Two seconds. Gone. The ribs reminding the body of the rules, even in sleep.

It was good that you slept.

Something in me is alive. Something in me wants me alive. Something in me is willing to destroy everything near me to achieve that objective. But whatever lived inside allowed me to remember the consequence. The broken ribs, the blood, the shockwave. It allowed the guilt to remain.

Why?

Because guilt reinforced isolation. Because remembering the damage I caused reinforced the conclusion that I should be alone. It *wanted* me to remember the destruction because the memory served its purpose. Keep me isolated. Keep me away. Keep the protection's radius clear.

The counter-manipulation was elegant. It erased the understanding while preserving the fear. I knew I was dangerous but could not remember exactly WHY. I could not access the understanding that would allow me to *manage* the danger. I could only feel it.

A cage made of my own feelings. Maintained by the thing inside me.

I sat on a fallen tree and I held this realization for as long as I could. The meta-realization that the selective erasure was itself strategic. That what did this was not mindlessly deleting but carefully curating. That the thing inside me was intelligent or at minimum purposeful.

This realization also degraded. Within minutes. The meta-realization about the strategic erasure was itself erased. The irony of it was that what lived inside erased my understanding that it erased strategically. And what remained after all the degradation was a feeling. Held at the level where the voice operated. At the level where the direction existed. At the level that the erasure could not fully reach.

It did not feel like an enemy.

The feeling was simple. It was the residue of what was left behind after the filtering removed everything else. The feeling did not explain the mechanism. It did not identify the thing. The feeling said only that it was not foreign. Not an invader. Something that had always been there and would always be there and that knew me from inside.

At the edge of the wreckage, you stirred. Your eyes opened. Focused on me.

"You're here," you said.

"Yes." Still here. Both of us. The thing that lived in me and me.

You closed your eyes again. The breathing steadied. Sleep, not unconsciousness.
