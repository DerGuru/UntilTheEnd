# OLAF PRESENTS: UNTIL THE END — EMBERS

**Animated Short Film — Screenplay**

---

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Format:** "Olaf Presents" is a series of animated shorts (originally ~90 seconds each; this installment runs approximately 3–4 minutes) in which OLAF, a sentient snowman from the *Frozen* franchise, performs a one-snowman retelling of a story. He plays all the roles himself, using improvised props, exaggerated physicality, and his own enthusiastic misunderstanding of dramatic conventions. The tone is *Drunk History* meets children's theater — sincere, chaotic, and unexpectedly moving.

**Animation Style:** Full 3D CGI, consistent with *Frozen 2* visual quality. Olaf's performance takes place on a makeshift "stage" he has built himself. When he acts out scenes, the camera stays on him — there are no cutaways to the actual story. The comedy comes from his physical transformations (posture, voice, props) and the gap between his cheerful delivery and the weight of the material.

**Runtime:** Approx. 3:30–4:00

---

## CHARACTERS

**OLAF** — A small snowman made of three snowballs, with stick arms, a carrot nose, and three coal buttons. Created by magic, alive, talkative, emotionally open to a fault. Speaks with childlike enthusiasm but occasionally stumbles into genuine profundity. Voiced with warmth and improv energy. He is the sole narrator, performer, and audience surrogate.

**SVEN** — A large reindeer. Present onstage as Olaf's "stage crew." Does not speak. Chews on things. Reacts with ear-flicks, snorts, and occasional alarmed stares. Functions as Olaf's straight man.

---

## SETTING

EXT. SNOW CLEARING — DAY

A small clearing in a snowy forest. OLAF has constructed a rudimentary stage: a flattened patch of snow with a single twig stuck upright in the center (his "tree"). A few snowballs are stacked nearby as future props. SVEN stands stage-left, chewing on a frozen branch. Faint wind. Soft light. The look of a backyard play put on by someone who takes it very seriously.

---

## SCREENPLAY

---

### TITLE CARD

A hand-painted wooden sign drops into frame, swinging on a rope. It reads:

**OLAF PRESENTS**
*"Until the End — Embers"*

The sign is slightly crooked. A snowflake drifts past it.

MUSIC: A single warm note — a celeste or music box — then silence.

---

### SCENE 1 — THE WOMAN IN THE FOREST

OLAF steps forward on his stage. He clears his throat (unnecessarily — he has no throat). He spreads his stick arms wide.

**OLAF**
Okay! So! There's a WOMAN. In a FOREST. And she's just — walking. She's been walking for FOUR THOUSAND YEARS.

*He pauses. Blinks. The number lands on him.*

**OLAF** *(quieter)*
Four thousand. That's —

*He counts on his stick fingers. Runs out. Tries the other hand. Gives up.*

**OLAF**
— a lot of birthdays nobody threw.

*Beat.*

**OLAF**
She doesn't eat. She doesn't sleep. She doesn't TALK to anyone. She just... walks and looks at trees and feels nothing.

*He tilts his head. Genuinely troubled.*

**OLAF**
Which sounds TERRIBLE. I feel things ALL the time! Like right now I feel EXCITED because I'm telling a STORY!

SVEN glances over, unimpressed. Keeps chewing.

---

### SCENE 2 — THE BOY

OLAF turns his back to the camera. When he turns around again, his posture has changed completely: shoulders hunched, arms swinging, a stick balanced on his back like an oversized basket. He bounces on his snowball feet. Stumbles. Catches himself. He IS the boy now — energetic, clumsy, LOUD.

**OLAF** *(as Wei)*
AND THEN — a BOY! He's twelve! He's collecting MUSHROOMS — the WRONG mushrooms, for his sick dad, and a GIANT PIG is about to eat him —

*He freezes mid-bounce. Eyes wide. Points at the twig-tree as if a boar has materialized behind it.*

**OLAF** *(as Wei, screaming)*
"WHY ARE YOU JUST STANDING THERE?!"

*Quick turn. He pulls himself upright, chin high, arms at his sides. Completely still. He IS the woman now. Ancient. Bored. One stick finger rises slowly.*

**OLAF** *(as Yun, flat)*
And she goes —

**SFX:** A soft, musical "pew" — like a harp string plucked once.

*OLAF flicks his finger. A burst of loose snowflakes erupts from behind the twig-tree (rigged practical effect — a small puff of powder snow released from above). The "tree" wobbles.*

*Silence. One snowflake drifts down.*

**OLAF** *(normal voice, awed)*
Pig: gone. Just — DUST. With one FINGER.

---

### SCENE 3 — THE NAME

*Rapid-fire character switches. OLAF bounces between positions — stage-right for Wei, stage-left for Yun — turning his body with each switch. The physical comedy accelerates.*

**OLAF** *(as Wei, bouncing stage-right)*
"WHAT'S YOUR NAME?!"

*Turns. Becomes Yun. Still.*

**OLAF** *(as Yun, flat)*
"No."

*Turns. Becomes Wei.*

**OLAF** *(as Wei, indignant)*
"'NO' ISN'T A NAME!"

*OLAF drops character. Turns to SVEN with a confiding look.*

**OLAF**
He has a POINT.

*SVEN snorts in vague agreement.*

---

### SCENE 4 — THE TEACHING

OLAF kneels. Gathers snowballs rapidly, lining them up in a row. He sorts them with exaggerated critical attention — sniffing each one, squinting, tossing them into piles.

**OLAF**
So the boy FOLLOWS her. Every morning. EVERY morning. He brings herbs — the WRONG herbs. She sorts them into piles.

*He tosses snowballs one by one.*

**OLAF**
Bad. Bad. Bad. Bad.

*Picks up the last one. Hesitates. Places it slightly to the side.*

**OLAF**
...less bad.

*He stands. Makes exaggerated air quotes with his stick arms.*

**OLAF**
She doesn't SAY she's teaching him. She's just... "correcting errors." For THREE MONTHS.

*Drops the air quotes. Looks directly into the camera.*

**OLAF**
That's called teaching, lady!

---

### SCENE 5 — THE CRISIS

MUSIC: Low tension drone begins. Subtle. Almost subliminal.

*OLAF straightens. His voice shifts — bigger, more dramatic. He gestures upward with one arm, pointing past the treetops.*

**OLAF**
And the whole time there's a GUY on a mountain — *(points up)* — doing a BIG POWER THING and the valley is DYING. The herbs die! The fish leave! The WELL turns YELLOW!

*He grabs the sides of his head with both stick arms.*

**OLAF**
It's winter but it feels like SUMMER which — very stressful for some of us!

*SVEN shifts nervously. OLAF climbs onto a small mound of snow. His posture changes again — becomes Wei. He puffs up his chest. His voice rises an octave, cracking with effort.*

**OLAF** *(as Wei, voice too high)*
"There's a spring. I know the route. Half rations. Three trips a day. I COUNTED THE FISH."

*He drops the character. Steps off the mound. Leans toward the camera and cups one stick hand beside his mouth.*

**OLAF** *(whisper)*
He counted the fish. He's TWELVE.

---

### SCENE 6 — THE WALLS

*OLAF begins stacking snowballs frantically — building a small wall on his stage. He packs snow with his stick arms, patting and shaping with manic energy. Snow flies everywhere.*

**OLAF**
And SHE — the ancient lady — builds WALLS. By HAND. Stone walls! Trenches! Six hours of digging! Her hands BLEED! She has BLISTERS!

*He stops. Looks at his stick arms. They don't bleed. He looks at the camera.*

**OLAF**
She could move MOUNTAINS with a thought but she digs with her FINGERS because —

*A long pause. His voice drops. Sincere.*

**OLAF**
— because she promised herself she wouldn't use her powers. And promises are IMPORTANT even when they're STUPID.

*He resumes building. Quieter now. Methodical.*

---

### SCENE 7 — THE CONGEE

MUSIC: The tension drone fades. Replaced by a single, warm woodwind note — an oboe or a shakuhachi. Gentle.

*OLAF stops building. He stands still. His whole energy changes — slower, softer. He mimes holding a bowl. Carefully. As if it's real.*

**OLAF** *(gently)*
And THEN his mom brings her CONGEE. That's rice soup. With ginger. And the ginger floats like little — little moons and —

*His voice wobbles. He catches it.*

**OLAF**
— and the mom says —

*Full character transformation. OLAF becomes the mother: perfectly still, arms at his sides, head slightly bowed. His stick fingers curl inward — miming cracked, working hands. When he speaks, it's barely above a breath.*

**OLAF** *(as the mother)*
"Stay."

*Silence. Three seconds.*

*OLAF drops the character. Stares at nothing. One stick hand reaches up and wipes across his carrot nose — the snowman equivalent of wiping tears.*

**OLAF** *(unconvincingly)*
I'm fine. Snow in my eye. HAPPENS ALL THE TIME.

*SVEN looks over. His ears flatten. Even the reindeer felt that one.*

---

### SCENE 8 — THE SKY

MUSIC: Sudden orchestral swell — brass and timpani. The celeste is gone.

*OLAF throws both arms skyward. He jumps.*

**OLAF**
THE SKY RIPS OPEN!

**SFX:** A deep, resonant BOOM.

**OLAF**
BOOOOM! Big wave of DEATH flying at the village! Her trench catches a THIRD of it! But the rest — the REST is coming and it's going to FLATTEN everything and everyone and the boy and his mom and the old guy who chews his cheek and the fish lady and —

*He stops.*

*All movement ceases. SVEN stops chewing. The wind dies. Even the ambient snow particles freeze in the air.*

*Slowly — very slowly — OLAF raises one stick arm. Open palm. Forward. His face is calm. The comedy is gone.*

**OLAF** *(quiet)*
She raises her hand.

*Beat.*

**OLAF**
One thought.

MUSIC: Cuts to absolute silence. No score. No ambient sound. Two full seconds of nothing.

*Then, softly:*

**OLAF**
The wave... stops. Everything stops. The DUST stops. The STEAM stops. The whole world just — holds its breath.

*He lowers his hand.*

**OLAF**
And then it's over. She dissolved it. With one thought. The sky heals. A bird sings.

**SFX:** A single bird call. Distant. Tentative. Real.

---

### SCENE 9 — THE COST

*OLAF sits down on his snow stage. Legs stretched out. Small. The energy has drained from him. His voice is quieter than it's been the entire film.*

**OLAF**
But the energy bounces back. Through the mountain guy's channels. Into his brand-new core. And she can see... little cracks forming. Like ice when you step on it wrong.

*He looks at the ground between his feet.*

**OLAF**
She broke him. By saving everyone else.

*Beat.*

**OLAF**
And the leftover energy floods into the BOY — through all those channels she carefully opened over three months — and he COLLAPSES. He sees the whole world glowing. He sees his mom and says —

*OLAF's voice drops to a whisper. The camera pushes in slowly — a gentle dolly toward his face.*

**OLAF** *(whisper)*
"Mama, you're beautiful."

*Two seconds of silence. His coal eyes glisten — an animation choice: a faint wet sheen across the surface, reflecting the light.*

**OLAF**
And then he falls.

*Beat.*

**OLAF**
She's there in one BREATH. Covers three miles like it's ONE STEP. Kneels beside him. Fixes him. Saves him.

---

### SCENE 10 — THE HILL

MUSIC: Returns. A single piano note, sustained. Then a slow, simple melody — major key, but with an ache in the intervals. Think Debussy played by someone who just learned what loneliness meant.

*OLAF stands. Looks up at the sky. The stage lights have shifted — bluer, cooler. Evening. Stars beginning to appear (animated particles drifting in the background).*

**OLAF**
And at the end she sits on a hill. Alone. Winter cold. Stars. The village is dark and quiet and still there. The boy is sleeping. The forest is broken but has edges. A bird tries its voice once.

*He pauses. A small, sad smile — the corner of his mouth curling upward, his coal eyes softening.*

**OLAF**
And she waits.

*Long beat. The smile holds. Then, gently:*

**OLAF**
She waited a really long time before that boy showed up. I think she'll wait a really long time after, too. But the nice thing is — now she has a REASON.

---

### SCENE 11 — THE REVIEW

*The tone snaps back. OLAF spins toward SVEN, arms wide, full energy restored.*

**OLAF**
FIVE STARS! It's about mushrooms and congee and a woman who pretends she doesn't care! SHE TOTALLY CARES!

*SVEN blinks slowly. One ear twitches.*

MUSIC: The piano melody resolves into a warm, major chord. Holds.

**SFX:** Soft snowfall begins. Large, slow flakes drifting down across the stage. OLAF takes a deep, satisfied bow — bending at the middle snowball. His carrot nose nearly touches the ground.

*SVEN sneezes. It's loud. A clump of snow falls off a branch.*

*OLAF straightens. Grins.*

SMASH CUT TO:

---

### END CARD

Black screen. The hand-painted sign drops back into frame:

**OLAF PRESENTS**

Below it, in smaller letters:

*Based on "Until the End" by [Author Name]*

The sign swings gently. One last snowflake drifts past.

MUSIC: Final note. Fade.

## TECHNICAL NOTES

**Total Dialogue:** Approx. 680 words (OLAF only). At Olaf's characteristic delivery pace (~160 words/min with pauses), this runs approximately 3:40–4:00 before music and silence beats.

**Sound Design:** The short relies heavily on contrast — Olaf's frenetic energy against sudden silences. The two-second dead silence after "One thought" is the emotional hinge. The bird call should be a real recording, not synthesized — it needs to feel like it wandered in from outside the film.

**Animation Notes:**
- Olaf's character switches should be physically distinct but never break his snowman anatomy. He doesn't grow arms or change shape — he changes *posture*, *speed*, and *voice*. The audience reads the character from body language alone.
- The "wet sheen" on his coal eyes during the "Mama, you're beautiful" whisper is a single-frame animation choice. Brief. Not a tear — snowmen don't cry. But the light catches differently for one second. The audience fills in the rest.
- SVEN's reactions are sparse and should be timed for maximum contrast: unimpressed during comedy, genuinely affected during the congee moment (ears flattening).
- The snowfall in the final scene should be the largest, slowest flakes in the film. They should feel like the world exhaling.
