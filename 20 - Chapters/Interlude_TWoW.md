# A Note Between Stories

You made it.

*Until the End* is done. Seven books. One road. One woman who couldn't die and a boy who couldn't stay dead. If you're reading this, you walked the whole thing. The herbs. The grief. The silence. The pears. All of it.

---

What comes next is not a sequel.

It's the same story — told from the other side of the room.

*The Weight of Watching* covers the same events, the same years, the same road. But you won't be inside Yun's head anymore. You'll be standing next to her. Seeing what she missed. Missing what she saw. Some chapters will show you scenes you've already read — and change what they mean. Some will fill in the gaps. Some will answer questions you didn't know you had.

The perspective shifts. First person becomes third. You'll follow four sets of eyes, marked at the start of each chapter:

- **Xu Ran** — The one who stayed.
- **Wei** — The one who couldn't.
- **The Pencil** — Someone who watched from a distance, and wrote it down.
- **The Hermit** — Someone who was never fully there.

And yes — the fox is still there. 

You don't *need* to have read *Until the End* first. But if you have, you'll see the seams. The moments where what you knew and what you're now learning don't quite line up — and then do.

Same story. Just sounds different from where they were standing.

— J.
