I woke to symmetry. The pines around the camp — six of them, mountain pines, the kind that grow where they please and answer to no landscaper — stood in a perfect circle now. Equal spacing. Equal height. Each trunk vertical, each canopy balanced, each root system arranged in the same radial pattern. Like perfect images of christmas trees. A circle so precise that a geometer would have wept.

"Those didn't stand like that," you said. You were already awake, sitting by the dead fire, trying to coax some flames and staring at the trees with the stillness of a man whose reality had been edited while he slept.

The trees looked like they'd always stood like that. The moss confirmed it. The earth confirmed it. The undergrowth between the trunks had grown in patterns consistent with decades of circular symmetry.

"No," I said. "They didn't."

But they had. Because I'd slept and in sleeping, the apparatus had continued its autonomous operation — shaping, adjusting, conforming the world to the architecture of my convictions without asking my permission, without requiring my consciousness, without needing any of the waking processes that I might have used to monitor or modify its behavior.

I shaped the world in my sleep.

The implication was — I sat with it. I shaped the world in my sleep. My unconscious beliefs operated continuously. Every moment. Every breath.

Twenty-four hours. Every single day of the year. Times a thousand. Times more. The multiplication was dizzying — the accumulated total of unconscious reality-edits produced by a consciousness that had never stopped believing for a single moment.

Every tree I'd ever walked past. Every stone I'd ever noticed. Every path I'd ever traveled. How much of what I saw was what existed and how much was what I believed existed? How much of the world was the world and how much was me?

"I can't turn it off," I said. "Not even in sleep."

You looked at the perfect circle of pines. Your face performed the micro-calculation that preceded your most careful observations. "That means everything you interact with —"

"Gets shaped. Yes."

"Everything you see."

"Yes."

"Everything you believe about."

"Yes."

"Even me."

"Yes."

We both looked at Xiǎohú. She lay between two of the perfect pines, sprawled on her side, one ear folded, one paw twitching in whatever foxes dream about. The only asymmetric thing in the clearing.

The circle of trees stood in the morning light, their symmetry beautiful and terrible and entirely unconscious. A woman's idea of what trees should look like, imposed on trees that had grown without asking, overwritten by a belief so quiet that it operated without waking the believer.

I couldn't turn it off.

The realization was a refinement of everything before it. But the trees made it physical. The trees made it visible. The trees stood there in their impossible perfection and testified, with the mute authority of wood and bark and root, that the woman sleeping among them had reshaped them in the night.

"The sect—" you started.

"Is coming."

I could feel them. Since I knelt in the river, the feeling had sharpened — the sense of their presence, the awareness of their cultivation signatures, the weight of a hundred-plus cultivators moving through the mountains with the patience of collectors approaching a source.

They were closer than they'd been.

They were always going to come. The anomalies I produced fed them. Had been feeding them. Would continue feeding them as long as I existed and they existed within range of my believing.

There was no hiding. There was no running. Millennia of shaping — tree by tree, stone by stone, night after night — had touched everything within reach. The excess of that shaping was their food.

The equation had one solution.

I didn't like the equation. I didn't like the solution. 

But the solution was the thing I was approaching.

Xiǎohú stood up. Stretched — front legs first, then back, the full-body arc of a creature unburdened by equations. She shook herself once, trotted past the perfect pines without looking at them, and disappeared into the undergrowth.

Hunting. Probably.

We gathered our belongings and walked. South.
