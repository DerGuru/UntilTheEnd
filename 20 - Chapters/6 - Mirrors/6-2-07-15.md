Evening arrived. The fire crackled. The ritual of flames and food and the domestic ceremony that kept the world from collapsing into meaning.

You were whittling.

I hadn't seen you whittle before. You'd started sometime during the afternoon, pulled a piece of wood from the trailside, drawn your knife and begun shaping it with the careful, rhythmic motions of a man who needed his hands to be doing something so that his mind could do something else. The wood was pale. The shavings curled at your feet. The knife made small sounds. Scrape, scrape, the intimate whisper of blade on grain.

I walked, you carved. I pushed away, you shaped.

I watched you carve and pulled out the notebook.

The second one. The one you'd built for me when the forgetting started — clean leather, larger handwriting, headers and sections, a backup of a person who was losing herself. I'd read it every morning for months. And somewhere along the way, I'd started adding my own pages. My observations. My hand, my syntax, wedged between yours.

I turned past your sections — the headers, the summaries, the careful architecture of someone else's record of my life — to where my own entries began. One per page. Space left beneath each for observations I'd never added.

*It intervened again. The external force — whatever protects me — acted to prevent the fall. I did not ask for this. I did not invoke this. It simply occurred, as if something outside my control decided that I would not be harmed.*

External force. As if the protection came from outside. As if something separate from me — a guardian, a mechanism, a god — had reached in and adjusted reality to keep me safe.

I tore the page from the binding. Along the spine. The sound was small — a whisper, a sigh, the intimate violence of paper leaving the book it belonged to.

Xiǎohú's head came up. She'd been dozing by the fire, but the sound of tearing paper brought her over in a quick trot, paws silent on the earth. She sat down a pace away, watching.

I laid it on the ground beside my knee.

Next page.

*Something shields me. I do not understand its nature or its motivation. The shielding is consistent. It activates in moments of physical danger and produces outcomes that redirect or eliminate the threat.*

Something shields me. The grammar of displacement. The subject removed, the agent obscured, the self written out of its own story.

I tore it out. Laid it beside the first.

Next.

*The healing was instantaneous. The wound closed between one breath and the next, without cultivation technique, without medicinal application, without any mechanism I can identify. Conclusion: an external factor is responsible for accelerating tissue repair. The factor's origin and nature remain unknown.*

An external factor. Out. Down.

Fourth. Fifth. The rhythm settled: read, tear, lay. Read, tear, lay. You carved, I dismantled and the evening fire burned between us. You looked at me, but didn't comment. Didn't ask what I was doing or why or whether I was sure.

Xiǎohú had moved closer. She sat at the edge of the page-row, her head tilting left as each new sheet joined the line. When the fifth page went down, she sniffed it. Looked at me. Her ears flattened briefly — not alarm, something more like disapproval. As if paper on the ground offended her sense of order.

Sixth.

*The probability of surviving multiple lethal encounters without external intervention is statistically impossible. Whatever protects me is consistent, powerful and specifically tailored to my survival. I hypothesize a guardian entity, possibly linked to my cultivation origin.*

Guardian entity. I almost laughed. The investigator investigating herself and concluding that someone else was responsible. Out. Down.

Seventh. Last.

*I do not know what protects me. I do not know why. I know only that it exists, that it is separate from me and that its intentions, if intentions they are, have consistently aligned with my preservation. For now, this is enough. Understanding can wait.*

Understanding can wait.

Out. Down.

Seven pages on the ground beside my knee. Seven careful, analytical records. The handwriting was steady. The observations were accurate. The conclusions — *external force, guardian entity, unknown factor* — I didn't believe them anymore. Whatever was happening, it wasn't separate from me. It had something to do with me. Beyond that, I couldn't say. Not yet.

But the old explanations were wrong. I could feel their shape and the shape didn't fit. Too neat. Too convenient. Too much like something designed to keep me from looking further.

The wall had built that shape. Had constructed a framework that placed the source safely outside me. The wall hadn't just blocked memories. It had built a grammar. A way of thinking that made certain questions unaskable.

I picked up the first page and put it in the fire.

The flame took it. The paper curled, blackened, became ash, became nothing. The irreversible nothing of a thing that had been destroyed. The flame flickered, a small hitch in combustion, a micro-hesitation, as if the fire had tasted the paper and found it bitter.

Or maybe just fire being fire.

The remaining pages went next. One by one. Into the flames. Each one a conclusion I no longer trusted. Each one burning. Each one gone.

Xiǎohú watched the pages curl. Her nose twitched at the smoke, ears forward, tracking each sheet's trajectory from my hand to the fire with the focused attention of a creature who found the destruction of perfectly good objects deeply suspect.

You carved. The shape in your hands was emerging, not recognizable yet, not a thing you could name, but a shape. A form resolving from formlessness.

"Done?" you said. Not looking up.

"Done."

The fire burned. The notes were ash. The old narrative was dead — the external force, the guardian entity, the unknown factor, the something that shields. Dead. Burned. Gone.

The new narrative was coming. The space was ready.

I watched the fire consume the last strip and felt the precise, ceremonial weight of a lie being buried.
