Night.

The kind that reminds you what darkness means. Complete, total, undiluted by moon or stars or the ambient glow of human habitation. The fire had burned down to coals, orange and pulsing and the heat had retreated to the circle of stones. Your sleeping form was a shape beside it, breathing the slow, deep breath of someone who had learned to sleep in places that didn't want him.

I couldn't sleep.

I knew the insomnia of anxiety, the mind spinning on its axis like a wheel that couldn't find friction. But this was different. My body was still. My thoughts were quiet. The calm that had been building for days was at its deepest, its most settled, its most complete. I lay in the dark. Empty. Vessel-empty. The emptiness of a container that has been scoured down to the glaze.

I got up.

I simply rose, the way the tree had appeared and the water had reversed. A change that happened and then had not. I was lying and then I had always been standing. 

I walked to the edge of our camp. Beyond the coals' glow, the darkness was absolute. Xiǎohú raised her head, gold-lit eyes catching a light source I couldn't identify. She watched me. Expectantly. The way you watch someone approach a door they've been circling for days.

I knelt.

The ground was cold. My knees found it and the cold came through the fabric of my trousers into my skin and into the bone beneath. The cold was real. The cold was the crack's gift. The sensitivity, the heightened awareness of temperature that had started with *I'm cold* and had never fully receded. The cold was a reminder. The cold was a signal. The cold was the wall's way of saying — you want to feel something? Here. Feel this.

I put my hands on the ground.

Palms flat. Fingers spread. Just hands on earth. The original gesture. The first thing a child does when they're placed on the ground. Teach down, touch and feel. Establish the fundamental relationship between one self and the surface.

The earth vibrated.

This was external. Physical. The ground beneath my palms was moving, a frequency so low that I felt it more than heard it. More understood than perceived. The vibration traveled through the soil, through the rock beneath the soil, through the geological layers beneath the rock, down and down and down to wherever the earth's trembling started.

Something was speaking.

Something older than language. Something that existed before the concept of communication itself. A frequency. A tone. A sustained note that the earth had been holding since the earth began and I — with my hands on the ground, my knees in the cold, the calm at its deepest and the wall at its thinnest — I could almost hear it.

Almost.

The vibration reached me. The frequency arrived. The tone was there, present, accessible. And then came a gap. A space between receiving and understanding, between hearing and comprehending, between the signal and the interpretation. Like hearing a word in a language you almost speak. Its meaning was right there. Almost. Just beyond the edge of understanding. Just past the threshold where perception becomes knowledge.

I pressed my palms harder into the earth. The vibration intensified. The tone deepened. The almost-hearing became almost-understanding. It carried images. The hands again, my hands, hands in light, creating, but closer now, clearer, more detailed. I could almost see what the hands were making. I could almost see —

The headache arrived.

Sharp and clean. A single, focused point of pain driven through the center of my forehead like a nail.

The self-protection seized.

My hands came off the ground. Involuntary. The wall asserting its authority with a force it hadn't needed before.

The vibration stopped. The tone stopped. The images imploded, contracting to a point and then vanishing. The almost-hearing collapsed into not-hearing and the almost-understanding collapsed into not-understanding. And I was on my knees in the dark with cold palms and a headache and the taste of blood mixing with the taste of metal.

I'd bitten my tongue. During the seizure my jaw had clenched and my teeth had found my tongue. I spat. Blood on earth. Dark on dark.

Xiǎohú was standing closer now. Half a meter away. Her amber eyes were at the level of my face and in them I could see — I thought I could see — a reflection. A reflection of something that wasn't present. A light that had no source.

The metallic taste was stronger than it had ever been. No longer a film. A flood. My mouth tasted like iron, like copper, like the inside of a bell that had been rung too hard. My teeth ached. My jaw ached. My entire skull resonated with the aftermath of the vibration, the echo of the tone, the residual frequency of whatever the earth had been saying.

I'd almost heard it.

Almost.

The *almost* was going to kill me. The reaching and the not-grasping. Close enough to feel the shape, and then, at the moment feeling was about to become seeing — shut.

But it had been closer than before. The wall had needed more force.

I — was getting closer.

The headache faded. Slowly, dispersing rather than disappearing, spreading from a point into a field, thinning until it was background rather than foreground. I sat back on my heels. My palms were cold and dirty. The earth was quiet.

You had woken and decided not to show it. Your breathing was even, measured, the rhythm of sleep or the performance of sleep. Either way, you stayed still. Either way, the morning would come and I would not tell, because some things needed to be held alone before they could be shared.

You seldom pushed.

Xiǎohú lay down beside me. Close, her fur against my knee, her warmth against the cold, her eyes still carrying that sourceless reflection. She didn't sleep. She watched the darkness and enjoyed the petting.

She looked at me expectantly. Like she was waiting for me to try again.

I would. But not tonight. Not with the headache's residue and the blood's taste still in my mouth. But soon. Tomorrow. The night after. I would put my hands on the earth and I would feel the vibration and I would hear the tone and eventually I would push past the almost.

The calm settled deeper. The metallic taste faded to its baseline. The night continued, dark and complete and indifferent.

In my chest, behind the crack, something hummed. Quiet. Patient. Waiting.

*Almost*.
