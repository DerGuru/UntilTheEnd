# Pre-Author Note — Trending

---

**A/N before we begin:**

So. This happened.

*Until the End* has been popping on and off the Trending list for about a week now. I keep refreshing the page like someone checking whether the stove is still on. It is. Then it isn't. Then it is again.

I genuinely don't know what to say, so I'll say the true thing:

This story is almost 300 chapters long. It has an herb-sorting training arc. It has a grief arc that lasts an entire book. The MC spends three chapters buying pears and getting a robe repaired. The most powerful being in the world struggles with exact change at a fruit stall. If you're here at Chapter 45 of Book 5, you didn't stumble in. You *walked* here. Deliberately. Through all of it.

That's not nothing. That's actually kind of everything.

Some of you have been here since Yun killed a spirit boar with a flick and pretended she didn't care about a hungry kid. Some of you found this last week and apparently decided that sleep was negotiable. Some of you left reviews that I've read more times than I'll ever admit. Some of you recommended this story to friends with the energy of someone trying to explain a fever dream — "No, listen, it's xianxia but she's *sad*, and there's a kid, and he collects the wrong mushrooms, and then you'll cry in a chapter about congee, just *trust me*."

You did that. You put this story on that list. Not algorithms. Not marketing. You, telling someone else that this weird, slow, sad, occasionally funny cultivation novel about an immortal woman who can't die and a boy who couldn't live was worth their time.

Thank you. For reading. For staying. For the comments that make me stare at my screen. For trusting the process through the quiet parts. For caring about these characters the way I care about them.

The story continues. East, still, always east.

— J.
