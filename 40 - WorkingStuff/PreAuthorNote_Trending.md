So. This happened.

Until the End has been on the Trending list for about a week now. I keep refreshing the page like someone checking whether the stove is still on. Not beliebig, what I can clearly see. I Even went so far as to check, if the browser serves me a cached version of the page. And I hope this is still true, when you could read this (which is currently tomorrow).

I genuinely don't know what to say, but thank you.

This story is already more than 300 chapters long. It has a training arc about herbs. It has a grief arc that lasted for almost an entire book. And the most powerful being in the world struggles with exact change at a fruit stall, because the world is currently too much for her. If you're here at the end of Book 5, you didn't stumble in. You walked here. Through all of it.

That's not nothing. That's actually kind of everything.

If you reached this point in the story, you have been here since Yun killed a spirit boar with a flick and pretended she didn't care about a hungry kid. You stayed through the quiet parts where nothing exploded and the tension was a woman walking through forests or mountains.

One of you even left a review and I am very grateful for that. A handful of you left ratings. Most were kind, one I cannot take seriously (nobody reads more than 250 chapters to find out, that it‘s worth only 1 star) and one didn't like it — which is fine. Not every story is for everyone. This one found some people anyway. You.

Some of you may have recommended this story to friends, which I am also grateful for. I know that word of mouth is the most powerful way to find readers, and I am honored if you told someone else about this story.

Some of you left comments. A few of those pointed out weaknesses I could fix — and did. Those comments made the books better. Thank you for that. Especially @Kobina, @Gravelton and @Doop.

You did that. You all who read this. And who'll read this until the end? Sorry, but that pun was too good not to drop. You put this story on that list. Not algorithms. Not marketing. You! By thinking that this weird, slow, sad, occasionally funny novel about an immortal woman who can't die was worth your time.

Thank you. For reading. For staying. For the comments. For trusting the process through the quiet parts. For caring about these characters.

This concludes Fractures but there are still two more books to go and a mystery to discover.

Let's find out what happens next.
