# Pre-Author Note — Trending

---

**A/N before we begin:**

So. This happened.

*Until the End* has been popping on and off the Trending list for about a week now. I keep refreshing the page like someone checking whether the stove is still on. It is. Then it isn't. Then it is again. I hope this is still true, when you could read this (which is currently tomorrow).

I genuinely don't know what to say, but thank you.

This story is more than 300 chapters long. It has a training arc about herbs. It has a grief arc that lasted an entire book. And the most powerful being in the world struggles with exact change at a fruit stall, because the world is currently too much for her. If you're here at the end of Book 5, you didn't stumble in. You *walked* here. Through all of it.

That's not nothing. That's actually kind of everything.

If you reached this point in the story, you have been here since Yun killed a spirit boar with a flick and pretended she didn't care about a hungry kid. You stayed through the quiet parts where nothing exploded and the tension was a woman walking through forests or mountains. 

One of you even left a review and I am very grateful for that. A handful of you left ratings. Most were kind, one I cannot take seriously and one didn't like it — which is fine. Not every story is for everyone. This one found some people anyway. You.

Some of you may have recommended this story to friends, which I am also grateful for. I know that word of mouth is the most powerful way to find readers, and I am honored if you told someone else about this story.

Some of you left comments. A few of those pointed out weaknesses I could fix — and did. Those comments made the books better. Thank you for that. Especially @Kobina, @Gravelton and @Doop.

You did that. You all who read this. And who'll read this until the end? You put this story on that list. Not algorithms. Not marketing. You! By thinking that this weird, slow, sad, occasionally funny cultivation novel about an immortal woman who can't die and a boy who couldn't live was worth your time.

Thank you. For reading. For staying. For the comments. For trusting the process through the quiet parts. For caring about these characters the way I care about them.

---

This concludes Fractures but there are still two books to go and a mystery to discover. 

Let's find out what happens next.
