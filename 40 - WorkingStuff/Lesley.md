The prose is beautiful but the story hurts deeply.  The main character is stuck in trauma and unable to react effectively to the problems she finds herself in.  

You find yourself trying to protect yourself from it rather than open up to it.  

It's missing a kind of emotional balance between pain and joy.  The descriptions are beautiful but they don't balance out the pain delivered.  And it's like a painting by Francisco Goya or Caravaggio that has been stripped of even the smallest highlights...the contrast between grief and anything else is lost...everything is grief and trauma.

But it shoots itself in the foot because grief is deeper and more meaningful in contrast to something.

Her story is just prolonged hesitation instead of progression. She stays in her stupor for half a novel...barely moving.

The problem is her inertia for half a book. She's too inert. Instead of giving the reader a sense of hard-earned accomplishment towards something, you're providing a sustained weight on the reader.

I'm not asking you to get weirdly motivational at the end of each scene. God knows that's a horribly overused stylistic device but she's not even planning for any kind of future...so the novel reads like a slow decent into some kind of suicide. Even the name of the novel seems to be hinting that nothing will get better. The boy will die or she will die. But before that death happens, where is the life?

So when she finally stops neglecting the kid with her weird silences...will it even be believable?

And if the neglect never ends, then why write a story about it? It's not even a story because the main character never changed.

There could be a one sentence explanation for the stupor rather than a backstory.

Maybe:

- She can't save them because her actions would ultimately lead to their deaths? If so you need to explain it. And reinforce her plans for how she might navigate this.

- They are actually bad people and she has come to see justice done on them. Also needs an explanation.

- maybe she loves the cultivator and would have to kill him to save the town...also needs an explanation and an attempt at circumventing the doom the town faces.

-etc

Every possible reason for sitting on her fingers and not at least evacuating or attempting to evacuate the town needs at least a few sentences of explanation

Otherwise you're only writing half the story and expecting it to satisfy as much as a full story. We read the story of a lady sitting and doing nothing and you secretly know that it means something.

That's not fun. You end up giving us whole chapters that don't move the story ahead at all ...they just reinforce that she's stuck.
