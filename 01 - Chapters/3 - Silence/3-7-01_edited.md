# Chapter 54

Morning. Grey again.

The settlement existed. Damaged — the east wall cracked where qi-blasts had hit, the south-west corner charred where fire had burned, two roofs caved from the shockwave of Wei's discharge. But standing. The barriers hummed. The protection signs glowed in the grey light, faint but functional.

Wei did not exist.

---

The garden. His garden. Three pots in a row. Clay, unevenly shaped — he'd made them himself, pressed and dried in the sun behind the house. The blade fragment at the edge. The two grey-blue stones he'd found interesting for reasons he'd explained once and that I had not retained.

The herbs. The dock grass: wrong. The nettle-cousin: wrong. The lemongrass: right, by accident. The one success in a row of failures that he'd treated no differently from the failures because equal attention, equal water, equal stubbornness — that was his method.

They were alive. The herbs. Green in the grey morning, growing in pots made by hands that were now torn and still and cold in the dirt outside the east gate.

The herbs grew. He didn't.

---

Lao Chen's workshop. Across the path. The door: open. Not forced — open, the way it had been left by someone who left quickly and who left the door because the door was irrelevant.

Inside: the workbench. Clean. Organized. Tools in their positions — hammer here, tongs there, files arranged by size. The forge: cold. Not recently cold — the coldness of a fire out for hours and not returning.

The grindstone: wet.

Still wet. Used recently enough for the water to not have evaporated. He was here. Recently. Working. Sharpening. And then he stopped. And the stopping was the leaving.

Not dead. Not killed. Gone. Evacuated, or departed, or removed himself from a place that contained a dead boy and an empty forge and a woman whose proximity killed grass.

The wet grindstone: evidence of his last act. The stone said: I was used. The user said: I am elsewhere.

---

Footsteps. Outside. The weight and rhythm of someone who walked heavily because stopping was not an option.

Frau Huang.

She came around the side of the house. A bowl in her hands. Ceramic. The same kind she'd used for years — practical, unchipped, the cookware of a woman who maintained her kitchen the way she maintained her children: loudly and completely.

Rice. Vegetables. Simple.

She set the bowl at the door.

She did not knock. Did not call. Did not shout — and the absence of shouting was the thing, because Frau Huang shouted. That was her nature, her method, her characteristic. The Loud Mother. The woman whose volume was the settlement's weather.

She was silent.

Behind her: Shen. Her son. Twelve. The boy who didn't hear well and who had followed Wei through the gate and who had been pushed backward by hands that were now torn. The pushing had saved him. He stood here — alive, present, shaking.

He was quiet. The boy who didn't hear: quiet. For the first time. Not the quiet of someone choosing not to speak but the quiet of someone whose capacity for sound had been altered by proximity to a core shattering.

He had seen it. Fifteen paces. Wei's discharge. The ugly, raw, non-golden light. He had seen it and the seeing was inside him now, in the place where sound used to live.

Frau Huang looked at the door. At the bowl. At the empty space that Yun would have occupied if Yun were occupying spaces.

She stood.

The expression on her face: not gratitude, not grief. Something else. Someone who understood — not the qi, not the cultivation — but the mathematics. The simple arithmetic of: one died so that another lived. And the one who died was fourteen. And the one who lived was hers.

She stood there with that arithmetic on her face and the arithmetic was unpayable and the unpayable was: I know.

Then she went. Back the way she came. Her son following. Their footsteps receding.

The bowl at the door. Steam rising. The temperature between hot food and cold morning producing visible condensation that would diminish and diminish and stop.

Inside: I had not moved. For hours. The house was a room. The room contained absence. The absence was shaped like a person who was not in it.

The bowl grew cold.
