# Chapter 33

The qi source.

Northeast. The mountain slope — an hour's hike through forest that was sick now, the trees showing the stress in their canopy, leaves curling inward with the defensive posture of vegetation that was protecting itself from something invisible by making itself smaller.

Wei led. Four settlers behind him — the supply carriers, the ones he'd selected for the first group. Adults. Middle-aged. Following a boy up a mountain because the boy was the one who knew where the mountain hurt.

I followed. Behind the group. The position of observation that was also the position of reserve — far enough to be background, close enough to be available, the spatial compromise of principle. Let him lead. Be ready.

The cave.

Small. A fissure in the rock face, barely wide enough for one person, descending at a twenty-degree angle into darkness. The qi-source: inside. The convergence point — the node where underground energy streams met and distributed — the valley's qi-heart. That heart was arrhythmic now. The arrhythmia was the cause, and the cause was what needed fixing.

Wei stopped at the entrance. Looked back at the settlers.

"Wait here."

"Wei—"

"It's narrow. And the qi-pressure inside is going to be strong. Anyone without cultivation will get sick."

He was right. The corridor would be saturated — the compressed qi of a destabilized source in a confined space. For ordinary bodies: migraine, nausea, vertigo. For cultivators: manageable. Barely.

He looked at me.

The look.

The look that was the question — not will you help, but will you let me. The look that contained two requests simultaneously: one, the permission, the implicit ask for the blessing that was not a blessing but a release, a loosening of the tie that connected his actions to my approval. And two: the trust. The quiet trust of someone who was about to walk into danger and who wanted to know that the person behind him was there and that the there-ness was steady.

I could have gone in. Could have walked past him, descended into the cave, identified the disruption in seconds, corrected it with a fraction of my capacity, walked out. Done. The settlers would have gone home. The fields would recover. The headaches would stop. Everything fixed, everything stable, everything: Yun handled it.

And Wei would have stood at the entrance. Watching. Having been bypassed. Having been rendered irrelevant by the efficiency of someone whose efficiency was millennia deep. That depth made everyone around her unnecessary. And unnecessary was the thing Wei had spent his life being — the wound I was healing. Healing required this: letting him go in.

"I'll be at the entrance," I said.

Zögern-Beat. The hesitation that was philosophy made flesh — the held breath of someone who could act and was choosing not to act. The choosing was the principle: he can do this, he must do this, this is the thing the teaching was for.

He nodded. Turned. Descended.

The darkness swallowed him — incrementally, the gradual absorption that happened when light yielded to curvature and curvature yielded to depth and depth was where he was going and where I was not.

I stood at the entrance. The settlers behind me. The forest sick around us. The mountain containing the source and the source containing the problem and the problem now containing my boy.

I could feel him. In the cave. His qi-signature — bright, distinctive, the signature that I could find in any crowd and any cave and any continent because finding him was the function and the function was the reflex and the reflex was permanent.

He moved deeper. The signature: steady. Descending. Approaching the node. I tracked his progress the way I tracked everything — clinically, continuously, with the practiced detachment that wasn't detachment but a mechanism for converting terror into data.

Twenty minutes.

He reached the node. I felt it — the sudden increase in ambient qi-pressure as the source responded to his presence, the convergence point recognizing a cultivator in its space and the recognizing being the interaction and the interaction being: the work.

He began.

The stabilization — qi channeled into the disrupted node, finding the imbalances, the energy-tilts, the deep dislocations that had caused the surface symptoms. Layer by layer. Methodical. For a fourteen-year-old: extraordinary. The kind of work that trained specialists performed after years of study, that required sensitivity and control and the patience to correct without overcorrecting and the patience being Wei's least natural quality and the least being the triumph because he was doing it anyway, doing it despite his nature, doing it because the situation demanded it and the situation's demand was louder than his nature's preference and the louder was the proof.

I counted.

His core. During the stabilization. While his qi was extended into the source, while his control was at maximum, while his body was conducting more energy than it had ever conducted:

The first twitch. Light. A stumble in the signal — his core's output flickering, the smooth sine wave catching briefly, recovering. Like a foot missing a step and correcting. Barely noticeable.

Noticed.

I gripped the cave wall. Stone under my fingers. Cold.

He continued. Deeper into the work. The source responding — slowly, incrementally, the energy patterns beginning to realign. He was doing it. He was actually doing it.

The second twitch. Stronger. His core's output pulsing — not flickering but PULSING, the overcorrection of a system that was compensating for the first instability by adding force and the adding being the problem because adding force to an unstable system was like bailing water with a bigger bucket: effective temporarily, damaging structurally.

I felt my nails against the stone. Against my palms. Against the hard surfaces that my body found when my body needed to hold onto something and the something was physical because the physical was available when the metaphysical was not.

He was finishing. The source: stabilizing. The patterns: aligning. Almost done. Almost—

The third twitch. The strongest. His core FLARED — bright, hot, the full-intensity discharge that happened when a system exceeded its governance parameters. Like a gong struck too hard: the note was correct but the vibration was excessive, the resonance lingering, the after-ring persisting beyond where the after-ring should have stopped.

The gong continued.

And Wei finished. The source: stable. The patterns: aligned. The valley: saved.

He emerged.

From the cave. Into the light. Dusty, sweating, his face pale with exertion. But standing. But grinning.

"Done," he said.

The settlers — they felt it. The immediate change. The qi-pressure lifting. The headaches receding. The air: lighter. The bodily relief that happened when the world went from wrong to right and the right was the boy and the boy was standing in the entrance of a cave and grinning.

They cheered. Not organized cheering — the spontaneous, involuntary vocalization of relief that emerged from people whose pain had stopped. The stopping was the joy, the joy was the sound, and the sound was his name.

Wei. Wei. The boy who'd fixed the earth.

He stood in the middle. Surrounded by grateful adults. Old hands on his shoulders, voices thanking him, eyes looking at him with the raw expression that gratitude produced in people who had been afraid and were no longer afraid.

He smiled. Not the grin — the smile. The quieter, deeper version. The one that was not performance but presence. The smile of someone who had done something real. The evidence was the feeling: I matter.

Then: his hands.

I saw them before he did. The tremor — both hands, bilateral, the fine post-exertion vibration that was not normal post-exertion vibration because normal post-exertion vibration was coarse and asymmetric and this was fine and symmetric and the symmetry was the diagnostic and the diagnostic was: core-mediated, not muscular.

He balled his fists. The trembling stopped. The deliberate suppression of a symptom through the application of conscious muscular override — clenching as containment.

"Just tired," he said.

To himself. To no one. The phrase that was becoming a refrain. The lyric of denial that repeated at higher frequencies. Their frequencies I was tracking.

He didn't notice the tremor. Or he noticed it and filed it under: normal. Because normal was the category that his fourteen-year-old psychology assigned to things that happened after effort and effort was the explanation and the explanation was sufficient because sufficiency was the thing he needed and the thing he needed was the thing he accepted and accepting was easier than investigating.

The settlers hugged him. Thanked him. Started the walk down the mountain.

I followed. Behind. With the count — three twitches during stabilization, each stronger than the last — and the hands — trembling, bilateral, core-mediated — and the phrase — just tired, just tired, the refrain of a boy whose body was speaking a language he couldn't hear.

Qi-Clock, Stage 2. Confirmed.

The gong was still ringing.
