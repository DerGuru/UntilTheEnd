# Chapter 60

The question remained.

Not answered — that was the point. Not answerable — that might have been the larger point. The question existed in the specific category of questions that were not designed for answers but for carrying, and the carrying was the weight, and the weight was the function, and the function was: to drive.

It would drive me. Into whatever came next, into the search, into the investigation that the question demanded — not of the world, not of the qi-architecture, not of the laws of cultivation, but of myself. Of whatever I was. Of the protection that was mine and that was not his and whose not-being-his was the crime and the crime was the accusation and the accusation was directed at: me. At whatever part of me was the mechanism. At whatever part was the dao or the curse or the fundamental condition that selected for my survival and against his.

But not today.

Today was: standing. In forest. With a question.

The wind moved through the trees. The branches shifted. The specific sound of a forest breathing — the creak of wood, the rustle of leaves, the small sounds that constituted the forest's background and whose background was the world's way of saying: I am here and I am not listening but I am here.

I stood.

The brown circle: steady. Three paces. Not growing, not shrinking. The equilibrium of an emission that had stabilized because the emission was tied to something and the something had reached its floor and the floor was: this. This level. This radius. This amount of dying.

I would carry the question. Into the next thing. Into whatever came after this forest, after this rain, after this grey series of days that constituted the aftermath of a death that had no parallel and whose no-parallel was the weight and the weight was the question and the question was the thing I would carry because carrying was the only verb available.

Whatever protects me — why not him.

The answer existed. Somewhere. In the architecture of whatever I was and whatever this was and whatever the mechanical reason was for the selectivity of protection that selected me and rejected him. The answer existed and the answer was findable and the finding would be the search and the search would be mine and the search would produce the answer and the answer would be—

But not today.

Today, the question stood. Alone. Without answer. In a forest. In rain that had stopped. In the brown circle of a woman whose grief killed grass and whose question killed something else — the specific comfort of not-knowing, the specific peace of assumption, the specific safety of believing that the universe's indifference was general rather than targeted.

It was targeted. The protection was targeted. At me. And the targeting excluded everyone else.

And the exclusion was the architecture.

But not today.
