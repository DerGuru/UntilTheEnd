## CRITICAL PART-WIDE ISSUES (read before per-chapter audit)

Before the chapter-by-chapter breakdown, three systemic problems must be flagged because they infect **every single chapter** and would cause redundant notes if listed seven times.

### â›” ISSUE A: "Specific" Tic â€” 69 occurrences across 7 chapters

The word **"specific"** appears **69 times** in ~10,000 words. Average: ~10 per chapter. This is the single most damaging stylistic defect in the Part. StyleDNA Don't #1 explicitly flags "particular/specific" as a tic to avoid. Additionally, "particular" appears once (Ch6 L7).

The construction is almost always identical: **"the specific [noun] that [clause]"** â€” e.g., "the specific coloration of plant matter," "the specific logistics of colony operation," "the specific atmospheric quality," "the specific gesture of someone removing debris." It functions as a verbal filler that masquerades as precision while adding nothing. Cut 90% of them. Where precision is actually needed, use concrete detail instead of the label "specific."

### â›” ISSUE B: "And the X was Y and the Y was Z" Recursive Chains

A dominant syntactic pattern across all 7 chapters: cascading definitional clauses where each noun is redefined by the next. Examples:
- "whose no-longer-alive-ness was a radius and the radius was mine"
- "wanting was the cable and the cable was disconnected"
- "the mechanism was all that remained"
- "the boundary was the fact and the fact was the border between the livable and the not-livable"
- "the evidence was: I had been here before"

This device creates a hypnotic, looping quality that *could* serve the "abgeklemmt" state â€” the mind stuck in definitional spirals because it can't move forward. **Problem:** It's used so relentlessly (multiple times per paragraph, every chapter) that it stops being a stylistic choice and becomes the only syntactic mode. The prose has one gear. Vary the pattern. Use it for key beats (1-2 per chapter max), cut it from routine description.

### â›” ISSUE C: Modern/Technical Register as Default Vocabulary

The word **"system"** appears **20+ times** across the Part. Additional modern vocabulary used as *default narration* (not as rare meta-leaks): "data," "mechanism," "biomechanical," "cable," "processing," "file," "archive," "environmental data," "biological operation," "organisms," "thermal signature," "hydraulic network."

Per StyleDNA: Modern metaphors are only permitted as **Gedanken-Echos** â€” max **1-2 per book**, as meta-leaks from Yun's suppressed knowledge. Using "system," "data," and "cable" as the go-to narrative vocabulary across every chapter burns through the meta-budget in the first Part and normalizes what should be uncanny.

Per Rule #41: While "System" (capitalized, referring to THE System) is what's formally banned, saturating the text with lowercase "system" as a narrative word creates exactly the confusion the rule exists to prevent. Replace with cultivation-world vocabulary or concrete imagery.
