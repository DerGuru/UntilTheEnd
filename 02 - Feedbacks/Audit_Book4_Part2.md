## CRITICAL SYSTEMIC ISSUE: THE "SPECIFIC" CRISIS

**159 occurrences** of the word "specific" across 10 chapters. Average: **~16 per chapter.** This is not a tic ‚Äî it's a structural crutch. The construction "the specific [noun] of/that [relative clause]" has become the prose's default explanatory mechanism. It replaces actual description with labeling.

Example of the pattern (every chapter has 10-20 of these):
> "the specific clarity of someone who spoke to be heard"
> "the specific deterioration that indicated weeks of travel"
> "the specific rhythm of someone matching pace at a deliberate distance"
> "the specific exhaustion of engagement"

**Verdict:** This is StyleDNA Don't #1. **80-90% of these must be eliminated.** Most can simply be deleted ("the clarity of someone who spoke to be heard" works identically). Others need rewriting into concrete sensory detail rather than categorical labeling.

## CRITICAL SYSTEMIC ISSUE: MODERN REGISTER CONTAMINATION

**51+ occurrences** of modern/clinical/technical register words that violate Rule #41's spirit and StyleDNA ¬ß5 (no modern register):

| Word | Occurrences | Examples |
|------|-------------|---------|
| system | ~20 | "olfactory system," "emotional system," "filing system," "analytical system," "cultivation system," "system restoring default settings" |
| data | ~12 | "data point," "data's location," "Every day was data" |
| filed/filing | ~6 | "filed: radius tighter in cold," "the filing system did not specify" |
| processing | ~4 | "background processing," "processing information" |
| cable | ~4 | "the disconnected cable," "the cable was still severed" |
| mechanism | 1 | "the specific mechanism for caring" |
| program | 1 | "the specific acoustic program" |
| calibrated | 1 | "the disconnection was calibrated" |
| default settings | 1 | "system restoring default settings" |
| configuration | 2 | "specific configuration of limbs" |
| systemic | 2 | "systemic shock" |

These words collectively create a **computer-science narrator**, not a ten-thousand-year-old woman who thinks in geological and natural metaphors. "The system rerouted to avoid the overloaded node" reads like a network diagnostics log. "Data, received, filed, not processed" is a database entry.

**Verdict:** Replace with Yun's established register ‚Äî natural phenomena, erosion, geological metaphors, bodily sensations. "The disconnected cable" ‚Üí "the severed root." "System restoring default settings" ‚Üí "the silence refilling the space where noise had been."

## CRITICAL SYSTEMIC ISSUE: POST-SHOW ANALYTICAL CASCADE

Nearly every emotional beat follows this pattern:
1. **Show** (good): physical detail, body sensation, environmental reaction
2. **Analytical cascade** (bad): 3-7 sentences explaining what the showing means in categorical terms

Examples:
- Ch10 (4-2-03): Qi jolts, leaves curl (GREAT showing) ‚Üí then 8 sentences dissecting "the break" using terms like "named category that emotional frameworks provided" and "data's location"
- Ch13 (4-2-06): Almost-smile (GREAT showing) ‚Üí then "The specific micro-expression that preceded the facial configuration of amusement" ‚Äî this EXPLAINS the showing, killing it
- Ch14 (4-2-07): Irritation detected (good) ‚Üí then "Irritating was a category. Irritating required the detection of preference‚Ä¶" ‚Äî three sentences of category theory

**Verdict:** Trust the showing. Cut 50-70% of the analytical follow-ups. The reader doesn't need Yun to reverse-engineer her own emotions through a classification taxonomy. Rule #22/#24: the reader is intelligent.
